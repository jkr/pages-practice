1. Introduction

Across Two Centuries

  • 1840-1928
  • A life spent in both the 19th and 20th centuries
  • Trained as an architect. Themes of excavation and renovation present throughout his novels.
  • Lived long enough to see four film adaptations of his novels

Across Two Forms

  • Originally wanted to be a poet.
  • Last novel, Jude the Obscure, in 1895.
  • For last 30 years of his life, exclusively a poet
  • Truly great novelist and truly great poet: perhaps unique in the language
  • “Nobody has taught me anything about writing since Thomas Hardy died.” (Ezra Pound)

Uniformitarianism

  • Darwin: Origin of Species (1859)
  • Influenced by geologist Charles Lyell: “The present is the key to the past”
  • Change happens very gradually over time.
  • Change is still occurring.
  • We can still see the past in the present.

we now seldom hear the agencies which we see still at work, spoken of as trifling and insignificant, when used in explaining the excavation of the deepest valleys or the formation of long lines of inland cliffs. Natural selection acts only by the preservation and accumulation of small inherited modifications, each profitable to the preserved being; and as modern geology has almost banished such views as the excavation of a great valley by a single diluvial wave, so will natural selection banish the belief of the continued creation of new organic beings, or of any great and sudden modification in their structure. (Origin)
