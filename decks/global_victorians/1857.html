1. Background

A more resolute, indefatigable pioneer never wrought amidst rocks and dangers. Firm, faithful, and devoted, full of energy, and zeal, and truth, he labours for his race; he clears their painful way to improvement; he hews down like a giant the prejudices of creed and caste that encumber it. (Charlotte Brontë, Jane Eyre)

Before 1857

  • James Broun-Ramsay, 1st Marquess of Dalhousie. Governor-General of India (1848–1856)
  • Improved logistics and transportation
  • Educational reform (particularly female education)
  • Annexed large portions of Indian territory to British rule
  • “Doctrine of Lapse”

Before 1857

  • Annexation of Punjab (1849)
  • Koh-i-noor diamond

2. The Rebellion

Sepoys

  • Indian Soldiers
  • In the Bengal Army, largely higher-caste soldiers
  • Grievances about pay and treatment
  • Fear of loss of religion and caste
  • The rumor of the cartridges

Mangal Pandey

  • March 29, 1857
  • Attacked officer, called for men to rebel
  • Executed

The Taking of Delhi

  • April 24, 1857: Refusal to use cartridges
  • May 9, 1857: Court-martial
  • May 10, 1857: Outbreak of revolt, march to Delhi
  • Bahadur Shah II (82 years old)

Spread through India

  • The Siege of Cawnpore (known for atrocities)
  • The Siege of Lucknow

3. Responses and Interpretations

I wish I were Commander-in-Chief in India… I should do my utmost to exterminate the Race upon whom the stain of the late cruelties rested; and…raze it off the face of the Earth. (Charles Dickens)

However infamous the conduct of the sepoys, it is only the reflection, in a concentrated form, of England’s own conduct in India…There is something in human history like retribution; and it is a rule of historical retribution that its instrument be forged not only by the offended, but by the offender himself. (Karl Marx)

The causes which had produced discontent among all classes might be ranged under three heads: first, our forcible destruction of native princes; next, our disturbance of the settlement of property ; third, our tampering with the religion of the people. These changes began about 1848, when it was necessary that the revenue of the Government should be increased. (Benjamin Disraeli, Speech to Parliament)

Nationalism of the modern type was yet to come; India had still to go through much sorrow and travail before she learnt the lesson which would give her real freedom. (Jawaharlal Nehru, First PM of India)

End of East India Company Rule

  • Government of India Act (August 1858)
  • Beginning of the British Raj

Firmly relying ourselves on the truth of Christianity, and acknowledging with gratitude the solace of religion, we disclaim alike the right and desire to impose our convictions on any of our subjects. We declare it to be our royal will and pleasure that none be in anywise favoured, none molested or disquieted, by reason of their religious faith or observances, but that all alike shall enjoy the equal and impartial protection of the law
